## Overview

This guide walks you through building an AI-powered legal document drafting and contract review system using OpenClaw. Designed for solo practitioners, small law firms, and in-house legal teams, this solution accelerates first drafts, flags risky clauses, ensures template consistency, and frees up attorney time for high-judgment work like negotiation and litigation strategy.

## Top 5 Skills You Need

- **Knowledge Base Skill** — Store your clause library, contract templates, legal precedents, and firm-specific style guides
- **PDF Generator Skill** — Produce formatted legal documents, engagement letters, and client-ready contracts
- **Browser Skill** — Research case law, regulatory updates, and jurisdiction-specific requirements in real time
- **Email Skill** — Send draft documents for client review and track revision requests
- **Google Sheets Skill** — Maintain a contract tracker with status, renewal dates, and key obligation deadlines

## Configuration Guide

### Step 1: Build Your Clause and Template Library

Assemble the building blocks for document generation:

- Standard clauses for your practice areas (NDA, MSA, employment, lease)
- Jurisdiction-specific variations for clauses like governing law and dispute resolution
- Firm-preferred language for common provisions (indemnification, limitation of liability)
- Red-flag clause patterns you always want flagged during review
- Client-specific customizations and negotiation history

### Step 2: Configure the Drafting Workflow

Set up how the AI generates first drafts:

- Intake questionnaire to capture deal terms (parties, dates, amounts, special conditions)
- Template selection logic based on document type and jurisdiction
- Variable substitution for party names, dates, dollar amounts, and defined terms
- Automatic cross-reference checking between sections
- Output in Word-compatible format for attorney review and redlining

### Step 3: Set Up Contract Review Analysis

Automate the tedious parts of contract review:

- Flag non-standard clauses that deviate from your template library
- Identify missing standard protections (cap on liability, termination for convenience)
- Highlight ambiguous language that could create interpretation disputes
- Compare incoming contracts against your preferred position on key terms
- Generate a summary sheet of key terms, obligations, and deadlines

### Step 4: Implement Quality Control Checkpoints

Legal work demands accuracy — build in verification:

- Every AI-generated draft must be reviewed by a licensed attorney before sending
- Maintain an audit log of all changes between AI draft and final version
- Run conflict-of-interest checks against your client database before new engagements
- Version control all document iterations with timestamps and editor tags

## Common Pitfalls

- **Never send AI-generated legal documents without attorney review** — The AI drafts, the lawyer approves; this is non-negotiable
- **Don't rely on AI for jurisdictional nuances** — Laws vary dramatically by state and country; always verify local requirements
- **Don't train on confidential client data without consent** — Ensure your knowledge base respects privilege and data protection rules
- **Don't automate court filings** — Procedural rules change frequently and errors can result in sanctions
- **Don't skip the conflict check** — AI can draft faster than you can catch a conflict of interest; always check first
