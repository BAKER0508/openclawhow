## Background

Rachel Dominguez was a former high school math teacher who left the classroom after eight years, frustrated by low pay and oversized classes. She had been tutoring privately on weekends, charging $60/hour, but could only handle about 15 students per week. When she discovered OpenClaw's Knowledge Base and Chat Skills, she saw a way to scale her teaching without cloning herself.

## What She Built

Rachel created "MathMentor AI," a WhatsApp-based math tutoring service for high school students preparing for SAT and ACT exams. The system uses OpenClaw's Knowledge Base Skill loaded with her own curated problem sets, step-by-step solution walkthroughs, and common mistake explanations. Students message the bot with a photo of a problem or a topic they are struggling with, and the AI walks them through the solution using the Socratic method — asking guiding questions rather than giving answers directly.

She added a weekly progress report feature using the Google Sheets Skill that tracks each student's practice volume, accuracy by topic, and improvement trends. Parents receive an automated email summary every Sunday evening.

## The Growth Path

Rachel launched with 10 beta students from her existing tutoring network, charging $49/month for unlimited AI tutoring access. She refined the AI's responses based on student feedback for two weeks, then opened enrollment through Instagram and local Facebook parenting groups. Word of mouth was her strongest channel — parents loved the instant availability and the progress reports.

By week six, she had 102 paying subscribers at $49/month, generating just over $5,000 in monthly recurring revenue. Her costs were minimal: $89/month for OpenClaw, $20 for WhatsApp Business API, and about 5 hours per week reviewing the AI's responses and updating the problem library.

## Key Takeaways

The replicability score is 4 out of 5 because the model is straightforward for anyone with subject-matter expertise. Rachel's edge was her deep understanding of where students get stuck — something that made her knowledge base far more effective than generic math content. The main risk is quality control: she still manually reviews flagged conversations where the AI's confidence was low, spending about an hour daily on this. She plans to expand into physics and chemistry by partnering with teachers in those subjects, using the same OpenClaw infrastructure.
