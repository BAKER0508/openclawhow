## Background

Sarah Whitfield is a partner at a four-attorney boutique law firm in Chicago specializing in commercial real estate and small business contracts. The firm was turning away work — not because of lack of demand, but because contract drafting and review consumed so much attorney time that they could not take on additional clients. Junior associates spent 60-70% of their billable hours on first-draft production and routine clause-by-clause review, work that was necessary but not where they added the most value.

## What She Built

Sarah used OpenClaw to create an internal AI legal assistant that handles two core workflows. First, contract drafting: attorneys fill out a structured intake form with deal terms, and the AI generates a complete first draft using the firm's own clause library stored in the Knowledge Base Skill. The library contains 400+ pre-approved clauses covering NDAs, commercial leases, operating agreements, employment contracts, and vendor agreements, all tagged by jurisdiction and risk level.

Second, contract review: when a client sends in a counterparty's contract, the AI analyzes it against the firm's preferred positions, flags deviations from standard terms, identifies missing protections, and produces a redline summary highlighting the top issues for attorney attention. The Browser Skill pulls recent case law references when the AI encounters unusual clauses.

Every AI-generated document goes through mandatory attorney review before reaching a client — this is a firm policy with no exceptions.

## The Results

In the first quarter of using the system, the firm's capacity for new client work increased by roughly 40%. First-draft production time dropped from 4-6 hours to under 45 minutes per contract. Contract review turnaround went from 2-3 days to same-day for standard agreements. This allowed the firm to take on 8-10 additional matters per month that they would have previously declined.

At the firm's blended billing rate, this additional capacity translated to approximately $12,000/month in new revenue. Associate satisfaction also improved measurably — they reported spending more time on negotiation strategy and client advisory, the work they found most engaging, and less time on repetitive drafting.

## Key Takeaways

The replicability score is 3 out of 5. The technical setup is manageable, but the real investment is building a high-quality clause library that reflects your firm's actual practice. Sarah spent six weeks curating and tagging clauses before the system was reliable enough for production use. She emphasizes that this is an augmentation tool, not a replacement: "The AI gets us to 80% in 10% of the time. The attorney's job is the last 20%, which is where all the judgment lives."
