## Background

David Okonkwo runs a three-location Nigerian restaurant chain called Sahara Kitchen in Houston, Texas. Each location was handling 80-120 delivery orders per day across phone calls, WhatsApp messages, and third-party apps. The phone ordering process was a constant headache: language barriers caused frequent order errors, hold times during dinner rush drove customers away, and he was paying three part-time staff members just to take phone orders across the three locations.

## What He Built

David implemented an OpenClaw-powered ordering system accessible through WhatsApp and his website. The AI agent presents the full menu with photos, handles customization requests (spice level, protein substitutions, combo builds), calculates totals with tax and delivery fees, and confirms orders with an itemized summary before processing. The Knowledge Base Skill stores the complete menu with ingredients and allergen information in both English and Spanish, serving Houston's diverse customer base.

Order confirmations flow directly to each kitchen's display screen via the Google Sheets Skill, which serves as a lightweight order management system. The Notification Skill pings kitchen managers when orders stack up beyond a threshold, and sends customers real-time status updates: order confirmed, preparing, ready for pickup, or out for delivery.

## The Impact

Within the first month, WhatsApp and web ordering captured 65% of total order volume that previously came through phone calls. Order errors dropped from an average of 8 per day across all locations to fewer than 1. David was able to reduce phone-order staffing from three part-time employees to one, saving approximately $3,000/month in labor costs.

Average order value increased 18% because the AI consistently suggests add-ons and combos — something phone staff did inconsistently. Customer complaints about wrong orders fell by 85%. An unexpected benefit was that the order data, now cleanly structured in spreadsheets, gave David clear visibility into which menu items performed best at each location, leading to a menu optimization that further improved margins.

## Key Takeaways

The replicability score is 4 out of 5 because the setup is straightforward and the ROI is clear for any restaurant doing meaningful delivery volume. The critical success factor was David's investment in building a thorough menu database with every customization option and allergen note. He spent two full weekends digitizing the menus and testing edge cases before going live. His advice: "Don't launch until you've tested every item on the menu with at least three weird customization requests."

## 背景

David Okonkwo在休斯顿经营着一家名为Sahara Kitchen的三店尼日利亚餐厅连锁。每家店每天通过电话、WhatsApp消息和第三方平台处理80-120个外卖订单。电话点餐一直是个头疼的问题：语言障碍导致频繁的订单错误，晚餐高峰期的等待时间赶跑了客户，而且他需要雇三个兼职员工专门在三个门店接听电话订单。

## 他做了什么

David通过WhatsApp和网站部署了一套OpenClaw驱动的点餐系统。AI Agent展示带图片的完整菜单，处理定制需求（辣度、蛋白质替换、套餐组合），计算含税和配送费的总价，并在处理前以逐项清单确认订单。知识库技能存储了包含英语和西班牙语两个版本的完整菜单、食材和过敏原信息，服务休斯顿多元化的客户群。

订单确认通过Google Sheets技能直接传送到每个厨房的显示屏，充当一个轻量级的订单管理系统。通知技能在订单积压超过阈值时提醒厨房经理，并向客户发送实时状态更新：订单已确认、准备中、可取餐或已配送。

## 成效

第一个月内，WhatsApp和网页点餐承接了之前电话订单总量的65%。订单错误从所有门店平均每天8个降至不到1个。David将电话接单人员从三名兼职减少到一名，每月节省约3000美元人工成本。

平均客单价提升了18%，因为AI会持续推荐加料和套餐——这是电话员工做得不够一致的事情。客户关于错误订单的投诉下降了85%。一个意外收获是，现在结构清晰地存储在电子表格中的订单数据，让David清楚地看到各门店哪些菜品表现最好，进而优化了菜单并进一步提升了利润率。

## 关键收获

可复制性评分4/5，因为搭建过程简单明了，对于任何有一定外卖量的餐厅来说ROI也很清晰。关键成功因素是David在搭建完整菜单数据库上的投入，涵盖了每个定制选项和过敏原标注。他花了整整两个周末将菜单数字化并测试边缘案例。他的建议："在菜单上的每道菜都用至少三个奇怪的定制要求测试过之前，不要上线。"
