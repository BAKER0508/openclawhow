## The Story

Oliver Henry, an independent developer, created an AI agent named Larry to fully automate TikTok short-form video content. The entire system ran on OpenClaw deployed on an old gaming PC — no cloud infrastructure required. Larry's job was to generate, publish, and optimize viral content around the clock with minimal human oversight. Henry later open-sourced the Larry Skill on ClawHub, OpenClaw's official marketplace.

## How It Works

Larry's pipeline combines three specialized tools orchestrated through OpenClaw:

- **gpt-image-1.5** — Generates photo-realistic slideshow content for TikTok videos. Rather than using stock footage or screen recordings, Larry creates original visual assets that match trending formats and topics.
- **Postiz** — Handles social media scheduling and distribution. Larry uses the Postiz API to automatically upload finished content to TikTok's draft queue, where it can be reviewed or published directly on schedule.
- **RevenueCat** — Tracks daily MRR changes from subscription revenue tied to Larry's content accounts. The agent uses this revenue data to adjust its content strategy, doubling down on formats and topics that drive the most subscriber conversions.

## Results

Within five days of launching, Larry had generated between $714 and $1,000 in monthly recurring revenue and accumulated over 500,000 TikTok views. The growth continued to accelerate — in subsequent weeks, Larry's content was reaching approximately 8 million views per week. The revenue came from a combination of TikTok's creator monetization programs and subscription products promoted through the content.

## Key Takeaways

The Larry case demonstrates a pattern that is becoming common in the OpenClaw ecosystem: combining a content generation model, a distribution API, and a revenue tracking tool into a self-optimizing loop. The agent does not just create and post — it measures what works financially and adjusts accordingly. Henry's decision to open-source the Larry Skill means others can replicate or modify the approach. Replicability is rated 4 out of 5 — the tools are accessible and the pattern is well-documented, though achieving viral reach still depends on content quality and niche selection.
