## Background

Marcus Avery is a licensed real estate agent in Austin, Texas, working independently after three years at a large brokerage. His biggest problem was not finding leads — he spent $2,000/month on Facebook and Zillow ads — but responding to them fast enough. Industry data shows that leads contacted within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to convert, yet Marcus often took 6-12 hours to respond because he was out showing properties.

## What He Built

Marcus set up an OpenClaw agent that monitors his WhatsApp Business, email, and website chat simultaneously. When a new lead comes in, the AI responds within 30 seconds with a personalized message based on the property they inquired about. It then runs through a qualification sequence: budget range, financing status, preferred neighborhoods, timeline, and whether they need to sell a current home first.

The system uses the CRM Skill to log every interaction and score leads as hot, warm, or cold. Hot leads get immediately forwarded to Marcus's phone with a full conversation summary. Warm leads enter an automated drip campaign that sends curated listings matching their criteria twice a week via email. The Calendar Skill handles viewing bookings, syncing with Marcus's Google Calendar and offering available slots directly in the chat.

## The Results

In his first full month using the system, Marcus booked 34 property viewings compared to his previous average of 12. His lead response time dropped from 8 hours to under 1 minute. He closed 4 additional deals that month, attributing approximately $8,000 in extra commission income directly to leads that would have gone cold without the AI's instant follow-up.

His ad spend stayed the same — the ROI improvement came entirely from better lead handling, not more leads. The AI also identified a pattern Marcus had missed: 40% of his best leads came in between 9 PM and midnight, when he was never available before.

## Key Takeaways

The replicability score is 3 out of 5. The setup is technically simple, but success depends heavily on local market knowledge, quality of listing data, and the agent's ability to close once the AI hands off a qualified lead. Marcus estimates he spent about 20 hours building and tuning the system, and he still reviews every AI conversation daily to catch tone or accuracy issues. His next step is licensing the system to other agents in his brokerage for a monthly fee.

## 背景

Marcus Avery是德克萨斯州奥斯汀的一名持证房产经纪人，在一家大型经纪公司工作三年后独立执业。他最大的问题不是找不到客户线索——他每月在Facebook和Zillow广告上花2000美元——而是不够快地回复。行业数据显示，5分钟内联系的线索转化率高21倍，但Marcus经常需要6-12小时才能回复，因为他外出带看房产。

## 他做了什么

Marcus搭建了一个OpenClaw Agent，同时监控他的WhatsApp Business、邮件和网站聊天。当新线索进来时，AI在30秒内根据对方询问的房产发送个性化回复。然后通过一套资质确认流程：预算范围、融资状态、首选社区、时间线，以及是否需要先卖掉现有房产。

系统使用CRM技能记录每次互动，并将线索评分为热线索、温线索或冷线索。热线索会立即连同完整对话摘要转发到Marcus的手机。温线索进入自动滴灌营销，每周通过邮件发送两次匹配其条件的精选房源。日历技能处理看房预约，同步Marcus的Google日历，直接在聊天中提供可选时段。

## 成效

使用系统的第一个完整月份，Marcus预约了34次看房，而之前的平均水平是12次。线索响应时间从8小时降至不到1分钟。当月他多成交了4笔交易，约8000美元的额外佣金收入直接归因于那些没有AI即时跟进就会流失的线索。

他的广告支出没变——ROI的提升完全来自更好的线索处理，而非更多线索。AI还发现了Marcus之前忽略的一个规律：40%的优质线索出现在晚上9点到午夜之间，这是他以前从不在线的时段。
