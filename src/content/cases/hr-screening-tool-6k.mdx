## Background

James Thornton spent 12 years as a talent acquisition manager at mid-sized tech companies before going independent as an HR consultant. His clients — startups and growing companies without dedicated recruiting teams — needed help screening high volumes of applications for engineering and product roles. James was spending 15-20 hours per week just reading resumes, a task that was necessary but left little time for the higher-value work his clients actually hired him for: interview design, compensation benchmarking, and hiring strategy.

## What He Built

James built an AI-powered screening pipeline using OpenClaw that processes applications from his clients' job boards and career pages. The Email Skill ingests applications as they arrive. The AI parses each resume, extracts structured data (skills, experience duration, education, project highlights), and scores candidates against a role-specific rubric stored in the Knowledge Base Skill.

Each client gets a customized scoring rubric James builds during onboarding: must-have qualifications, preferred skills weighted by importance, and automatic disqualifiers. The Google Sheets Skill maintains a live candidate pipeline for each open role, sortable by score, application date, and stage. Top-scoring candidates (above 75/100) automatically receive a personalized acknowledgment email and are advanced to the interview scheduling queue, where the Calendar Skill coordinates slots between the candidate and the client's hiring manager.

Candidates below the threshold receive a respectful rejection email within 48 hours — something most of James's clients never managed to do consistently before.

## The Results

James now serves 6 clients simultaneously, each paying $1,000/month for the screening service plus his strategic advisory. The AI handles approximately 800 applications per month across all clients. His time on resume screening dropped from 20 hours per week to about 3 hours of spot-checking and rubric refinement.

Clients report that interview-to-offer ratios improved by 35% because the candidates reaching the interview stage are better qualified. Time-to-first-interview dropped from an average of 12 days to 4 days. Candidate experience scores improved significantly — applicants consistently mention the fast, professional communication as a positive differentiator.

## Key Takeaways

The replicability score is 4 out of 5. The model works well for any HR professional with recruiting expertise who serves multiple clients. The key differentiator is the quality of the scoring rubrics — James spends 2-3 hours per role building these, drawing on his deep understanding of what actually predicts success in each position. His advice for anyone replicating this: "The AI is only as good as your rubric. If you can't articulate what makes a great candidate for a role, the AI certainly can't figure it out for you." He plans to scale to 12 clients by Q3 2026, which he estimates will bring revenue to $12K/month without hiring additional staff.
